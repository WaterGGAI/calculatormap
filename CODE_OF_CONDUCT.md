# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We want CalculatorMap to be a welcoming, practical, and respectful open source project. Contributors, maintainers, and community members should be able to participate without harassment, hostility, or gatekeeping.

We commit to making participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity or expression, experience level, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that help create a positive environment include:

- being respectful in disagreements
- assuming good intent while still giving direct technical feedback
- focusing on the problem, code, or workflow instead of the person
- welcoming contributors with different levels of experience
- sharing context, reproduction steps, and evidence when reporting issues

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, intimidation, threats, or personal attacks
- discriminatory jokes, slurs, or derogatory language
- trolling, deliberately inflammatory comments, or repeated bad-faith arguments
- publishing someone else's private information without permission
- sexualized language or unwanted attention

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, and other project-linked communication channels.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, do not open a public issue with personal or sensitive details. Use a private maintainer contact path instead, and include links, screenshots, or other relevant context when possible.
